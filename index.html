About Me

If there is one thing that I consistenly loved to do throughtout my life, that would be problem solving. In school, math and science classes were the best part of the school week (aside from lunchtime) because they required a logical and thorough thought process to solve problems. To me, History was about regurgitating dates and actions. You couldn't "fix" the past. English is a language where there are too many ways to say the same thing. But Math and Science...there was only one correct solution and one efficient way to get that solution. That required thorough thinking and meticulous, mind-numbing problem-solving methods.

Baseball felt the exact same way to me, especially being a pitcher, where every pitch and every play could have a different outcome and have an affect on the game. What pitch should I throw here? If he swings, was he early, late or did he take it? How did he take it? What pitch do I throw next if he does A, B or C? The constant thinking required to maximize my pitching, pitch count and flow of the game was everything I loved. And to me, software development and programming provides the same level of excitement. What's the most efficient and optimal way to code this program?
